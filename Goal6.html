Goal 6: Clean Water and Sanitation


Clean Water

For the United Nation's 6th goal, one of the targets is to have clean water and have it be accessible worldwide. Even though this is a worldwide problem, many people don't know about it's affects all over the world, and sometimes only think of this as an issue in distant countries. However water cleanliness is a problem right here in Canada.

Did you know that there are many First Nation communities in Ontario which do not recieve clean water, and even though this has been brought to the attention of our government, they haven't taken action about it? This is a very big issue in our country and has become a crisis within First Nation communities.

Even though this is such a big issue, not much is being done about it, and the federal government is instead trying to get First Nations communities to buy their own bottled water, even though clean water is a right.

Clean water throughout the world is important, and cities worldwide have been impacted by unclean water. When one does drink unclean water, especially over a period of time, they are much more likely to develop illnesses, poisoning, and cancer. Clean water is imperative for a human and is a big priority.




Sanitation and Accessibility

The second part of this goal is the sanitation of water, and this is very similar to clean water. Water sanitation is when unclean water is cleaned so that it becomes safe. As noted before, this is a big issue in First Nation communities, and it is also a problem worldwide. Unclean sanitation applies not just to water one may drink, but to water to bathe, or to cook with.

Diseases of all kind are dangerous, and while some spread through direct transfer (between two people), or through animals (such as mosquitoes), there is another way diseases can be transmitted. This is through the spread of disease in unsanitized water and these are known as waterborne diseases.

There are many types of diseases that can be transmitted through water, and some well-known ones are cholera, hepatitis A and polio. These can cause almost 500,000 deaths per year. Just because of water not being sanitized. Not to mention all the other effects of unsanitized water.

Another big issue in the world is water accessibility. There are places around the world which don't have access to any type of water services, and even if they did have access, the water isn't safe to drink. It's a big problem all over the world, and an urgent one that needs to be fixed.
